FOOD of GREENWICH

BREAKFAST LUNCH DINNER TOWN FAVORITE

Just a train ride away from New York City, the town of Greenwich is located on the water of Long Island Sound in southwestern Fairfield County. This New England town is known for its high-end stores, upscale dining options as well as its rich cultural scene. While Connecticut is not usually associated with impeccable cusine, Greenwich is an exception to this generalization. Greenwich Avenue, a mile long street lined with exquisit eatteries and resturants, offers locals a number of different dining options that always leaves customers satisfied. From French coffee shops and Italian ice cream parlours to Hibachi restaurants and diners, the food in Greenwich is hard to compete with.

Ada's Kitchen + Coffee

A chef-driven local eatery offering breakfast, lunch, and dinner to go, tasty treats, locally roasted artisan coffee...and, of course, candy. Ada's scratch-made seasonal food, featuring fresh ingredients from local farms and purveyors always leaves customers happy.

Rinaldi's Deli

Rinaldi’s Country Deli has been a popular Greenwich cornerstone for over thirty years. Sisters Michelle Rinaldi and Antionette Martinez think of and treat their customers as family, sharing a great love for the family business. Rinaldi’s offers a wide variety of foods ranging from breakfast sandwiches, salads, deli sandwiches, hot plates, and even provides catering services for events.

Le Fat Poodle

Le Fat Poodle possesses a quirky personality and an intentional design by owners Antoine Blech and Anshu Vidyarthi. The menu has French classics, but also includes q modern, eclectic bistro with international dishes such as Thai mussels and Korean sticky wings.

Sweet Peas Bakery

A family run business located in the town of Old Greenwich, Sweet Peas Bakery has everything from healthy to super tasty, from ice teas to lattes, homemade almond chocolate croissant to an avocado or açai bowls, salads with a glass of wine to our unique Pão de Queijo combos.

The Granola Bar

The Granola Bar; a modern diner. A place of no food rules and no judgements – at the intersection of healthy and indulgent. The Granola Bar is where you can have a kale smoothie on Monday and an avocado grilled cheese with bacon on Friday – and try everything in between.

L'escale Restaurant

The iconic, l’escale restaurant bar, or “port of call” for seasonal Mediterranean cuisine is designed to transport the ambiance of Provence to the Gold Coast of Connecticut. Two-hundred-year-old stone terra cotta tiles from Provence adorn the floors with a wood burning fireplace lending an unmistakable charm and beauty.

Upper Crust Bagels

Upper crust prides themselves on the freshness of their bagels that are baked on premises daily using only natural ingredients. Their business is family owned and operated in the seaside town of Old Greenwich, CT since 1994, and the pride of our generations shows through in every thing they do. Their menu selections include Kettle Boiled & Hearth Baked Bagels, Gourmet Spreads, Salad & Sandwiches.

Steam

Steam is a modern interpretation of Chinese and Southeast Asian cuisine. Using only the finest seasonal ingredients, Steam's chef-inspired menu offers healthy and innovative renditions of classic and popular Chinese and Southeast Asian dishes. Steam's mission is to offer wholesome food at reasonable prices in a relaxing and warm environment where colleagues, friends, and families can casually gather.

Box Car Cantina

Opened in 1994, Boxcar Cantina is family owned and operated, featuring an authentic Northern New Mexican cuisine, which moves beyond Southwest/Tex-Mex food. The resturant creates their delicious food by blending three distinct cultures together – Native American, Spanish and Anglo-European.
